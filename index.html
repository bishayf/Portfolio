About

Where two worlds
meet at the bedside.

I'm a Year 3 MBBS student at the University of Limerick, previously completing an Honours BSc in Psychology, Neuroscience & Behaviour with a Mental Health Specialization at McMaster University. Before medical school, I spent four years doing clinical and educational research — chronic pain, trauma, psychiatry education — and I haven't stopped since.

I'm drawn to the spaces where disciplines overlap: the psychiatric consultation that changes a patient's medical trajectory, the GP who sees the whole person, the ward team that actually talks to each other. My electives, my research, and the journal clubs I run are all reflections of the same instinct — that the best medicine is integrative, and that curiosity is the most important clinical skill.

I grew up between Egypt and Canada, and I live between Ireland and Ontario. Coptic Orthodox, Arabic-speaking, Egyptian-Canadian — I bring a perspective to clinical encounters that isn't easily categorized, and I've come to think that's one of the most useful things I have.

Outside the hospital, I shoot on 35mm film, run 60km every November for Movember, feed 125 people every Tuesday, and serve two church communities on two different continents. I'm a Liverpool supporter, a Raptors fan, and — in arguably my most impressive achievement — a two-time fantasy sports champion.

Research

Active Work

Social Media & Health

Asthma Content on TikTok

Cross-sectional content analysis of asthma-tagged TikTok videos using established instruments (mDISCERN, GQS) plus novel purpose-built coding tools I developed. First author; manuscript under editorial review.

Supervisor: Dr. Samir Gupta · St. Michael's Hospital / U of T

Mental Health Education

Mental Hygiene for Young Readers

A Frontiers for Young Minds article introducing positive psychology and mental hygiene to ages 12–15. Written with Dr. Kristin Delcellier. Currently under editorial review.

Co-author: Dr. Kristin Delcellier · McMaster Psychiatry

Trauma & Recovery

Resilience, Moral Injury & PTSD in Healthcare Workers

Senior Honours Thesis examining emotional and interpersonal resilience as protective factors against moral injury and PTSD in healthcare workers during COVID-19. Manuscript in preparation.

Supervisor: Dr. Margaret McKinnon · McMaster University

Quality Improvement

PPV23 Vaccination Audit

Led a retrospective clinical audit of pneumococcal vaccination uptake among 95 diabetic patients at Woodbrook Family Practice. Found 45% uptake against an 80% ICGP standard; recommended EMR-based recall systems.

Supervisor: Dr. Brendan Payne · Woodbrook Family Practice, Co. Cork
